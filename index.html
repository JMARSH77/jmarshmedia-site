Novels

The Portal

The debut novel from Kentucky author Jeremy Marsh asks what happens when technology lets us relive the moments we were never meant to keep. Set in a near-future Cincinnati still recovering from economic collapse, the story follows Jacob Hastings, a grieving tech founder who builds an AI system designed to let people step back inside their most powerful memories.

As Jacob descends deeper into the machine he created, he discovers the Portal doesn't just replay the past, it reacts to it. What begins as a desperate act of love becomes a battle between memory and truth, forcing Jacob and his family to confront what it really means to let go.

Equal parts speculative fiction and intimate family drama, The Portal grounds its futuristic premise in raw emotional reality.

Altered State

Coming 2026

Fifteen years ago, Isaiah Hale made a promise to a God he no longer answers.

The world did not end in fire. It ended in arithmetic: who gets to live, and who is left outside the wall. When the herd comes for the people he swore to protect, Isaiah holds the one crossing they cannot lose. What he does there will save them, and cost him the last thing he believed he was.

A harrowing, humane debut for readers of Station Eleven and The Dog Stars.

The Author

About Jeremy

Jeremy Marsh is a writer. Fiction, non-fiction, songs, the occasional YouTube project that takes three times longer than it should. If it involves words or something that could reasonably be called creative, he's probably already in.

Born and raised in Kentucky, Jeremy doesn't just rep the state; he means it. You'll find him at Kroger Field on Saturdays in the fall, hollering at referees who absolutely cannot hear him. Catch him on the right weekend and he might be at a baseball game, or a basketball game cheering on Big Blue, or somewhere in between with a bourbon in hand and a completely defensible opinion about all of it.

He describes himself as a nerdy jock, which confuses people until they spend five minutes with him. He reads constantly. He lifts consistently. The two have always coexisted without incident.

His writing is character-driven and character-obsessed. The decisions people make when no one is watching. The things left unsaid and the weight they carry. He's published one novel and has another in the works. More on that soon.

If you landed here, you're probably exactly where you're supposed to be.
