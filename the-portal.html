The Story

What the Machine
Remembers

In a near-future Cincinnati still recovering from economic collapse, Jacob Hastings has built something that shouldn't exist — an AI system called the Portal that lets people step back inside their most powerful memories. Not watch them. Live them.

Jacob is grieving. He tells himself the technology is for others. But the machine knows what it's for, and so does everyone around him. As he descends deeper into the architecture he created, the Portal begins to reveal something he wasn't prepared for: it doesn't just replay the past. It reacts to it.

What begins as a desperate act of love becomes a battle between memory and truth — forcing Jacob and his family to confront what it really means to let go, and whether some doors, once opened, should ever be walked through again.

Equal parts speculative fiction and intimate family drama, The Portal grounds its futuristic premise in the weight of the ordinary: a marriage under pressure, a son watching his father disappear into a screen, a city quietly becoming something no one voted for.
