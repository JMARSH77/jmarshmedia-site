DEBUT NOVEL

The Portal

A haunting tech-thriller of grief, surveillance, and one man's attempt to buy back a life he broke.

The story is about Jacob Hastings's return to Hastings Tower, where AR, AI, and fractured memory blur into a terrifying reality. The core tension lies in the morally fraught limits of technology and the price of redemption in a surveillance-driven system. The reader is left holding a complex question about consequence, guilt, and the fragile hope that lingers after a personal storm.

About

Jeremy Marsh

Jeremy Marsh writes character-driven fiction that blends emotional truth with cinematic tension. He's drawn to the moments where survival cuts against morality, exploring themes of consequence, grief, and the quiet weight of guilt. His prose is confident, grounded, and intensely human.

Marsh’s stories often take place in detailed, atmospheric settings, applying the pacing of a big-screen thriller to intimate, high-stakes personal crises. His work is ultimately about the fragile hope that lingers after a person breaks something important and the difficult, often lonely, price of earning redemption.

Official Biography

Jeremy Marsh: A Writer of Resonance and Place

Jeremy Marsh is an American novelist whose work blends emotional truth with cinematic grit. His stories cut to the bone, where survival meets morality and silence carries more weight than confession. Drawing from small-town realism and big-screen pacing, he writes fiction about the price of redemption, the burden of guilt, and the fragile hope that lingers after the storm.

His debut novel, The Portal (2025), is a tech-thriller that established his voice, and he is widely praised for his confident prose and the emotional depth he brings to complex human dynamics.

My Story

Why I Write

"I build characters, put them under pressure, and watch what they do. That’s why I write. I’m not chasing perfect people or clean morals. I want the moment where someone has to choose between survival and who they thought they were. I care about consequence, about what it really costs to keep going after you’ve already screwed something up. The story comes from that: take a person, break the comfort, make it real, and see if they can live with what they did."

— Jeremy Marsh
